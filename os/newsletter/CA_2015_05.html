Invitation to serve on Trust Committees
Trust committees are organized by the trust (board of directors) and comprised of trust members and old students who support the center by overseeing various aspects of operations. Many committee tasks can be done off-site. CVC trust committees include Accommodations, Administration, Development, Finance, Kitchen, Land Stewardship, Maintenance, Dhamma Service, Outreach, Safety and Technology.

Learn more about specific committee responsibilities at

Between-Course Dhamma Service Periods at CVC
June 7 - 12
June 21 - 24
July 5 - 8
July 19 - 22
August 2 - 5
There is much to do to prepare the center for every course. Between-course servers help with laundry, spot cleaning of accommodations, kitchen prep, etc. If time allows, they may also work on other center upkeep like maintenance, organizing, landscaping and deep cleaning.

The service periods between courses at CVC start at 9am at the end of a course (Day 11) and end at about noon at the start of the next course (Day 0).

Servers attend three daily group sittings during between-course service, and follow the Code of Conduct for Dhamma Servers https://www.dhamma.org/en/about/dscode.

Old Students can now register online to serve between courses. Please visit the course schedule page https://www.dhamma.org/en-US/schedules/schmahavana to apply online for a specific date. If you have been practicing other meditation techniques since your last course, please sit another 10-day course before serving.

Dhamma Service Interest Form
The center would appreciate knowing about your Dhamma service interests, skills, and availability. Please take a moment to indicate how you might like to participate by ﬁling out this online form.

Extended Sitting & Service Opportunities
Old students who are interested in developing their practice may stay at the center on a course-by-course basis to sit and serve. Those old students who would like to stay for extended periods at the center should be trying to establish their daily practice and trying to keep the five precepts in their daily lives. For more information, please email dhamma-service@mahavana.dhamma.org.

Dhamma Vaddhana Trust/Planning Weekend - May 30-31
All old students are invited and encouraged to attend the annual planning weekend at SCVC. This is an inspiring weekend and a great opportunity to strengthen one's practice, learn about service opportunities, meet with assistant teachers, and become connected with the center in a deeper way. During the weekend, there will be presentations about developments at the center and visions for the future. Committees including kitchen, accommodations, maintenance, technology, etc. will meet and make plans for the coming year. Old students are encouraged to join a committee and support the operations of the center. Servers will also be needed to help in the kitchen for the weekend.

The planning weekend will begin with a group sitting at 9am on Saturday, May 30, and end on Sunday, May 31 after the Trust business meeting and lunch around 1pm. Please apply online for the program through the course schedule. To support the program by helping with preparations, cooking, etc., please apply for part or all of the May 27-31 service period.

Dhamma Vaddhana Board of Trustees Meetings
Sunday: May 31
*Note: Timings earlier than usual due to annual planning weekend (see above)
8:00 – 9:00 Group Sitting
9:15 – 11:15 Trust meeting
11:30 - Lunch and Clean-Up

All old students are welcome to attend the monthly meeting of the Southern California Vipassana Center Trust, the forum where continuing operations and development of Dhamma Vaddhana are discussed. It is an excellent opportunity to learn about the center, hear reports from working committees, and become involved in serving in whatever way you are available.

Between-Course Dhamma Service Periods
May 18 - 20
June 1 - 3
June 15 - 17
June 29 - July 1
July 13 - 15
July 27 - 29

There is always much to do to prepare the center for the next course, and servers are needed for the between-course periods. Staying at the center between courses is a peaceful experience with fewer people on site and a more relaxed schedule. Servers attend three group sittings daily between work periods and follow the Code of Conduct for Dhamma Servers. Vipassana in Idaho and Utah

10-day course: May 27 - June 7 in Ketchum, Idaho
This course takes place in the beautiful mountains just north of Ketchum, ID.
We are in need of 5 full-time servers.
Giving service is a great way to strengthen your practice in daily life.
If you are able to serve this course either full-time or part-time, please apply online at:
http://courses.dhamma.org/en-US/schedules/noncenter/id.us

Pre-course servers are also needed for the course.
This course needs a food coordinator and food buyers as well as help with set-up. Training will be provided. Food buyers purchase food for the course in a bigger city such as Boise, Salt Lake City or Twin Falls, and then transport it to Camp Sawtooth (just north of Ketchum, ID) on either May 26 or May 27. All purchases for the course will be reimbursed.
Set-up for the 10-day course will begin at 4pm on May 26th and will continue May 27th morning.
If you are able to help with food coordination, food buying or with set-up please contact: info@id.us.dhamma.org
