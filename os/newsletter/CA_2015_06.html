Trust committees are organized by the trust (board of directors) and comprised of trust members and old students who support the center by overseeing various aspects of operations. Between-Course Dhamma Service Periods at CVC
July 5 - 8
July 19 - 22
August 2 - 5
August 9 - 12
August 23 - 26
There is much to do to prepare the center for every course. Between-course servers help with laundry, spot cleaning of accommodations, kitchen prep, etc. If time allows, they may also work on other center upkeep like maintenance, organizing, landscaping and deep cleaning.

The service periods between courses at CVC start at 9am at the end of a course (Day 11) and end at about noon at the start of the next course (Day 0).

Servers attend three daily group sittings during between-course service, and follow the Code of Conduct for Dhamma Servers https://www.dhamma.org/en/about/dscode.

Old Students can now register online to serve between courses. Please visit the course schedule page https://www.dhamma.org/en-US/schedules/schmahavana to apply online for a specific date. If you have been practicing other meditation techniques since your last course, please sit another 10-day course before serving.

Dhamma Service Interest Form
The center would appreciate knowing about your Dhamma service interests, skills, and availability. Please take a moment to indicate how you might like to participate by ﬁling out this online form.

Extended Sitting & Service Opportunities
Old students who are interested in developing their practice may stay at the center on a course-by-course basis to sit and serve. Those old students who would like to stay for extended periods at the center should be trying to establish their daily practice and trying to keep the five precepts in their daily lives. For more information, please email dhamma-service@mahavana.dhamma.org.

October 5 - 7

There is always much to do to prepare the center for the next course, and servers are needed for the between-course periods. Servers attend three group sittings daily between work periods and follow the Code of Conduct for Dhamma Servers. Vipassana in Idaho and Utah

Preparing for 10-day Course: Sept 14 - 25, 2015 in Idaho
This course takes place in the beautiful mountains just north of Ketchum, ID.
We are in need of 5 full-time servers.
Giving service is a great way to strengthen your practice in daily life.
If you are able to serve this course either full-time or part-time, please apply online at:
http://courses.dhamma.org/en-US/schedules/noncenter/id.us

Pre-course servers are also needed for the course.
This course needs a food coordinator and food buyers as well as help with set-up. Training will be provided. Food buyers purchase food for the course in a bigger city such as Boise, Salt Lake City or Twin Falls, and then transport it to Camp Sawtooth (just north of Ketchum, ID) on either Sept 13 or Sept 14. All purchases for the course will be reimbursed.
Set-up for the 10-day course will begin at 4pm on Sept 13th and will continue on the morning of Sept 14th (Day 0).
If you are able to help with food coordination, food buying or with set-up please contact: info@id.us.dhamma.org
